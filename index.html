Financial Resilience Index

A behavioral measure of financial health

Unlike static risk questionnaires, the FRI is a dynamic behavioral benchmark. It measures how you actually respond to financial stress — not what you say you'd do.

Security

Buffer adequacy, income stability, emergency preparedness, debt trajectory

Freedom

Life choice flexibility, financial independence, career change readiness

Pleasure

Discretionary spending insights, life enjoyment metrics, guilt-free zones

Research Foundation

Grounded in behavioral science

Our platform is built on decades of peer-reviewed research in behavioral economics, cognitive psychology, and financial decision-making.

01

Prospect Theory

People feel losses 2x more intensely than equivalent gains. Our system detects loss-aversion-driven decisions and reframes choices to reduce emotional bias.

Kahneman & Tversky, 1979
02

Nudge Architecture

Small changes in choice architecture produce outsized behavioral effects. FIONA delivers micro-nudges at the exact moment of financial decision-making.

Thaler & Sunstein, 2008
03

Financial Well-being Theory

Financial health is driven by behavior, not just income. Our FRI measures perceived security, capacity, and freedom as behavioral outputs.

CFPB Framework, 2015
